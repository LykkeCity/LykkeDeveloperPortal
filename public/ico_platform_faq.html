Frequently Asked Questions

What is an ICO?

ICO stands for “Initial Coin Offering” and it is the way of raising capital through a crowdfunding campaign with the use of crypto-assets as investment. A digital asset, the coin or the token, is issued for a purpose and are sold to raise money for the said purpose.

After the ICO process is done, the coins are traded on crypto-exchanges and market supply and demand decides on their fair pricing.

What is an ITO?

An ITO stands for the “Initial Token Offering”, the equivalent process of an ICO.

What is the difference between an ICO and an ITO?

The first generation of digital assets were the crypto-coins like Bitcoin, Litecoin etc.. Coins have been used as a digital medium of doing transactions in an elegant encrypted way. The next generation of digital assets is the tokens, which cover a broader purpose of use.

Is an ICO/ITO similar to an IPO?

The IPO refers to the “Initial Public Offering”, the process where a company becomes listed on an exchange. In this way, the public company offers its shares to the public for trading and the market decides on the price of the share. Some well-known companies which had a successful IPOs are Microsoft, Facebook, Snapchat etc.

The IPO’s purpose is limited to the shares of a company, but the ITO refers to a multi-purpose and multi-dimensional products, which are not limited to shares. The new products are the tokens, or the digital assets in general.

Is an ITO legal?

Most of the investments in ITOs are done through crypto-assets. Crypto-assets are not illegal, they are rather un-regulated. High risks are involved in such investments though.
